About This Star Map

This visualization uses the HYG star database (Version 4.2), which combines data from the Hipparcos, Yale Bright Star, and Gliese catalogs. It includes positions, apparent magnitudes, distances, and spectral classifications for thousands of stars.

Star Temperatures & Colors

Astronomers classify stars by spectral type (O, B, A, F, G, K, M), which is closely related to surface temperature. Hot O and B stars tend to appear blue, while cooler K and M stars appear orange-red.

What the Axes Mean

The horizontal axis shows Right Ascension (RA) in hours, and the vertical axis shows Declination (Dec) in degrees.
